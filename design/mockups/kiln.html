Why the KV cache exists
# Why the KV cache exists Autoregressive decoding recomputes attention over the whole prefix at every step. That's **O(n²)** work you already did. ## The trick - Keep the per-layer **K** and **V** tensors from prior tokens - Only compute Q, K, V for the *new* token each step - Attention becomes O(n) per step instead of O(n²) The cost moves to memory: cache size grows linearly with sequence length times layers times heads. That's why long context gets expensive.

So the key insight is that during autoregressive decoding you never need to recompute the keys and values for tokens you've already seen. You cache them. Every new step only produces one new query, one new key, one new value.

The attention operation then reduces from quadratic to linear per step, and the whole cost of generation shifts from compute into memory bandwidth — which is why on long contexts the bottleneck becomes how fast you can stream the cache…
